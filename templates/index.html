What are Carbon Credits?

Many projects around the world are reducing emissions or pulling greenhouse gases out of the atmosphere, we're able to fund some of them by buying Carbon Credits. These credits represent the amount of CO2e that the project is able to reduce or capture, with a single credit usually respresenting a tonne of carbon. We make things easier by purchasing credits in bulk on behalf of our users, giving you peace of mind with none of the hassle.

Are all Credits equal?

Carbon Offsetting is a new and booming industry so, unsurprisingly, standards vary. While there are many worthwhile projects reducing emissions in heavily polluting industries we think it's important to actually remove greenhouse gases where possible. We also believe it's best to do so close to home, that's why we purchase Carbon Credits from projects here in the UK that are certified by the Woodland Carbon Code or the Peatland Carbon Code; these environments naturally capture and store CO2, and as an added bonus they also help support biodiversity. It doesn't get much better than that.

There's another issue: Double Counting. Some cheeky offsetters will sell their Carbon Credits more than once! As each credit represents a specific amount of CO2e the resale of these credits means an unlucky customer has not balanced anything with their purchase. We will only buy ours from offsetters who retire serlialised credits, this is done on a global scale using an open ledger (blockchain) which means there can be no cheating. Hooray!
